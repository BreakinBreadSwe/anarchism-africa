# The Modern Colosseum — research dossier

**Subject:** the financial, political, social and environmental anatomy of a
**France vs Sénégal** men's football match, used as the lens for (a) a long-read
article and (b) the **FairPlay Thermometer** impact index.

**Compiled:** 2026-06-16. **Method:** six parallel web-research passes, claims
extracted with sources and confidence notes, contested figures flagged. Most
primary sites returned HTTP 403 to automated fetching this session, so a number of
precise figures rest on search-index summaries of named sources — these are marked
and **should be re-opened before publication**.

> **Fixture note.** France and Sénégal had not previously met in a senior men's
> *competitive* fixture; the live context is their **2026 FIFA World Cup** meeting
> (France's reported opening opponent). Match-specific sponsor/airtime numbers do
> not yet exist publicly, so the figures below are **transferable benchmarks** —
> the right orders of magnitude, not a scoreboard of this exact game. Where a
> number is an estimate or contested, it says so.

---

## Headline takeaways

1. **The spectacle is a media-value machine.** Sponsor exposure is no longer counted
   in raw "seconds on screen" — firms (Nielsen, Relo Metrics, GumGum) weight size,
   prominence and audience into a *media value*. A single top-flight match can throw
   off **£700k–£2m** of branding value for one partner ([Nielsen Sports][n1]).
2. **The audience is colossal but routinely mis-quoted.** FIFA's "5 billion engaged"
   and "1.5 billion final" are **cumulative cross-platform reach**, not simultaneous
   viewers ([FIFA][f5b]). France's 2022 final drew a national-record **24.08m**
   average on TF1 ([Advanced Television][at1]).
3. **The wage gap is the colonial story in miniature.** France's squad is worth ~3×
   Sénégal's (~€1.52bn vs ~€478m, [Planet Football][pf1]); a star earns more in two
   or three days of club salary than Sénégal's record AFCON bonus (~$135k/player,
   [ESPN][espn1]) pays for winning a continent.
4. **Most of the planetary damage is travel — and it scales down, not just up.**
   ~80% of EURO 2024's *audited* footprint was transport ([UEFA via edie][uefa1]);
   the 2026 World Cup's modelled fan-travel share is ~87% ([Inside World Football][iwf1]).
   At the grassroots, the measurable harm today is *vulnerability* — ~120,000 English
   matches cancelled a year to flooded pitches ([Sky Sports][sky1]).
5. **The youngest spectators are marinated in gambling ads.** One Premier League match
   showed **5,262** visible gambling messages, 91% on-screen during live play
   ([Univ. of Bristol][bris1]).

## §1 — Advertising & sponsor airtime economics

**How exposure becomes money.** "On-screen seconds" alone is considered inadequate:
a perimeter LED ribbon can fill the lower third in one camera angle and shrink to a
pixel strip in the next. Modern methods weight by size, clarity and prominence:
- **Nielsen Sports — "QI Media Value"** combines exposure quality/weight, audience size
  and cost-per-thousand; "Whitespace Valuation" scores in-stadium exposure on logo
  size/location, duration and number of brand "hits" ([Nielsen][n1]). *Confirmed.*
- **Relo Metrics** uses visual AI across broadcast + streaming + social, attaching
  viewership data; claims omnichannel captures "up to 80% additional value" vs
  broadcast-only ([Business Wire, 28 Nov 2023][bw1]). *Vendor claim — treat as marketing.*
- **GumGum Sports — "Media Value Percentage"** scores each exposure on clarity,
  prominence and share-of-voice ([Front Office Sports][fos1]). *Confirmed.*

**Concrete media-value figures:**
- Premier League club partners can earn **£700k–£2m in global media value per match**;
  halfway-line perimeter zones ~**£200k–£600k per match** ([Nielsen][n1]). *Estimate.*
- The **jersey** is the highest-value location — 38–54% of a club's total media-value
  exposure; kit providers for top-40 clubs earned **$70.6m** in QI value over one
  half-season (2017-18) ([Nielsen][n2]). *Confirmed, dated.*
- **Caveat (NBA analogy):** GumGum found **76–81%** of jersey-patch media value came
  from *social*, not the live TV broadcast ([Hashtag Sports][hs1]) — broadcast-airtime
  value alone *understates* total sponsor value. Use as analogy only (not football).

**Who dominates the two broadcasts:**
- **France/Ligue 1:** food & beverage, telecom, retail, betting — the league is titled
  "Ligue 1 McDonald's," with Betclic (betting) a strategic partner; distribution via
  Orange, Bouygues, Free, SFR, DAZN, beIN ([SportsMint][sm1]; [Broadband TV News][btn1]).
- **Sénégal/Africa:** **telecoms and betting**. Orange (via its stake in Sénégal's
  Sonatel) has backed the Sénégalese FA ~15 years and has been CAF's exclusive telecom
  partner since 2008 ([SportBusiness][sb1]). Betting brands now out-bid others: Betway
  took South Africa's league title sponsorship for ~£38.3m/3yr ([Inside World Football][iwf2]);
  **1xBet** sponsors AFCON and is top-of-mind in Sénégal ([SeneNews][sn1]).

**Tournament scale:** FIFA's 2019–22 cycle generated **$7,568m** total revenue,
**$1,795m** from marketing/sponsorship rights ([FIFA Annual Report 2022][far22], primary).
A World Cup sponsorship slot averaged ~**$63m** over the cycle ([GlobalData][gd1], estimate).

*Gaps:* no France-vs-Sénégal-specific airtime figure exists; no public per-match
perimeter-LED value for a World Cup match; the Orange/Sonatel–FSF deal value is undisclosed.

## §2 — Broadcast air rights & audience reach

**France — rights:**
- TF1 + beIN paid **€130m+** combined for the 2018 + 2022 World Cups ([SportBusiness][sb2]).
- **2026 + 2030 World Cup free-to-air rights went to Groupe M6**, not TF1 (which had
  held them since 1978); M6 airs 54 of 104 matches FTA, reportedly paying above the
  €130m 2022 cost ([FIFA][fm6]). **beIN Sports** holds the French pay-TV package.
- TF1 holds French men's national-team rights through 2028 ([SportBusiness][sb3]).
- *Context:* Ligue 1 domestic rights ~**€500m/yr** (DAZN + beIN), a deal that later
  collapsed ([France24][f24l1]).

**Sénégal & sub-Saharan Africa — rights:**
- **New World TV (Togo)** is the lead sub-Saharan rights-holder — World Cup 2022 and
  renewed for 2026 across ~43–46 territories incl. Sénégal ([FIFA][nwt1]).
- **RTS** (Sénégal's public broadcaster) holds FTA World Cup rights in Sénégal and won
  exclusive AFCON 2025 rights there ([SportBusiness][sb4]).
- **AFCON 2026** sub-Saharan rights: **SuperSport/MultiChoice** — whose parent is being
  acquired by France's **Canal+** in a ~$1.9bn deal ([Inside World Football][iwf3]).
- *Most African rights values are undisclosed* — a genuine data gap, not just unverified.

**Reach (read the caveats):**
- **2022 World Cup:** ~**5 billion engaged** globally — but this is broad cross-platform
  *engagement*, not viewers ([FIFA][f5b]); final ~**1.5bn cumulative** reach (~607m on
  home TV) ([FIFA/Sportcal][sc1]); average per match ~**175m** ([FIFA][far22b]).
- **France:** 2022 final = national-record **24.08m** avg / 81% share, peak 29.4m on TF1
  ([Advanced Television][at1]); EURO 2024 opener 11.25m — a ~10–15% decline ([Eurosport][es1]).
- **AFCON 2025** (Sénégal competing): **3.45bn cumulative** reach across beIN — a
  *broadcaster self-report*, cumulative ≠ unique ([Nat. Law Review][nlr1]). Treat as promotional.

> **Mis-quote watch:** FIFA's "5 billion" and "1.5 billion" are cumulative reach across
> TV + digital + out-of-home, not simultaneous unique viewers. Flag prominently in any copy.

## §3 — Player economics

**Club wages (media estimates — contracts private, present as ranges):**
- **Mbappé (France):** ~$100m/yr pre-tax at PSG pre-2024; Real Madrid base ~€31.3m +
  up to ~€30m bonuses ([Sportico][spo1]; [Goal][goal1]).
- **Sadio Mané (Sénégal):** ~£34m/€40m tax-free at Al-Nassr (~£650k/wk); was ~€22m at
  Bayern ([Sky Sports][sky2]).
- **Kalidou Koulibaly (Sénégal):** ~€30–34.7m gross/yr at Al-Hilal; ~£15m at Chelsea
  before ([Goal][goal2]).

**National-team bonuses:**
- **Sénégal, AFCON Jan 2026:** president pledged **75m CFA (~$135k)** per player to 28
  players (~$3.7m total) plus 1,500 m² land plots ([ESPN][espn1]). **Flag:** that title
  was reportedly disputed/overturned by CAF and the FA faced a bonus-management crisis —
  verify final status before relying on it.
- **Sénégal, AFCON 2021:** players reported rewarded ~$87k each plus honours and land
  ([Quartz Africa][qz1]). *Estimate.*
- **France, 2022 World Cup:** ~€500k per player from the run; ~€210k image-rights bonus
  — **single-outlet, page 403, UNVERIFIED** ([Get French Football News][gff1]).

**FIFA prize money:**
- **2022:** $440m pool — winner $42m, runner-up (France) $30m, group floor ~$9m
  ([NBC Sports][nbc1]). *Confirmed.*
- **2026:** raised to a record ~**$727m** team distribution (~$871m all-in incl.
  delegation/ticketing — don't conflate the two); champion ~$50m, minimum per team
  ~$12.5m ([Al Jazeera][aj1]; [CNBC][cnbc1]).
- **Structural fact:** FIFA pays *federations*, not players — the share reaching players
  depends on each association's negotiated split (France ~30% reported; Sénégal disputes).
- Separate **Club Benefits Programme**: ~$209m to 440 clubs for 2022 (rising to ~$355m
  for 2026) — money to clubs, not players ([FIFA][fcbp]).

**Performance moves market value** (Transfermarkt value = a crowd-sourced *estimate*,
not a fee — [peer-reviewed][tm1]):
- **James Rodríguez:** 2014 Golden Boot → Real Madrid ~€80m, up from ~€45m a year earlier
  ([France24][f24jr]).
- **Enzo Fernández:** 2022 Best Young Player → Chelsea £106.8m, months after a ~£10m move
  to Benfica ([Sky Sports][sky3]).
- **Édouard Mendy (Sénégal):** ~€32m around AFCON 2022, up from the ~€22m Chelsea paid
  ([footboom][fb1]). *Secondary — verify.*

**The gap, stated plainly:** France's squad ~€1.52bn vs Sénégal's ~€478m
([Planet Football][pf1]); a top player's club wage dwarfs the largest African-federation
tournament reward by orders of magnitude (analysis, not a single stat).

## §4 — Political / colonial / soft-power layer & the economics of hosting

**Colonial pipeline:**
- France colonised Sénégal until **1960**; Dakar was the capital of French West Africa.
- France's **2018 World Cup winners** fielded ~**14–15 of 23** players of African heritage
  from ~9 countries ([Andscape][and1]) — state as a range (definition-dependent).
- Patrick Vieira and Patrice Evra were both **born in Dakar** but played for France; Evra:
  "When you win with France you are a French player; when you lose... you get your
  Senegalese passport" ([Soccer Laduma][sl1]).
- Sénégal's 2026 squad in turn draws heavily on French-developed players (~up to 10 born
  or academy-formed in France — [Bolavip][bv1], approximate).
- The "Frenchness" debate runs from Le Pen's 1998 attack on the Zidane/Vieira/Desailly
  team to today ([Al Jazeera][aj2]).

**Soft power / prestige:** Sénégal's first AFCON (Feb 2022) brought a **national public
holiday** and a Dakar homecoming ([BBC][bbc1]); a second title (Jan 2026) brought another
holiday and an open-top parade ([France 24][f24a]) — *verify the contested final result.*

**Sportswashing:** states/corporations using sport to launder reputation and distract
from rights abuses ([MERIP][merip1]) — a contested analytic frame. Documented examples:
Qatar 2022, Saudi Arabia's 2021 Newcastle takeover, Gulf investment in European clubs
([ESPN][espn2]).

**Hosting — costs, debt, white elephants:**
- **South Africa 2010:** ~$2bn+ on stadiums, ~10× original estimates, inflated by
  construction-firm collusion ([PRX/The World][prx1]). Cape Town Stadium loses ~$6–10m/yr
  ([The Stadium Business][tsb1]) — build cost cited variously $255m–$600m (flag the spread).
- **Brazil 2014:** ~$13.3bn total; auditors estimated corruption at 30–40% of venue costs
  ([CBS][cbs1]); Brasília's stadium (~$900m) became an office/bus depot; Manaus (~$300m)
  hosts crowds under 1,000 ([Sports Illustrated][si1]).
- **Qatar 2022 — the most-misused number:** the famous **$200bn** (some say up to $300bn)
  is **decade-long national development** (metro, airport, port, hotels), per the **IMF**;
  **stadium construction was only ~$6.5bn** ([IMF][imf1]). Use $6.5bn for tournament-specific
  cost; the $200bn headline is real but mis-attributed.
- **AFCON:** Cameroon 2021's main stadium ~$284m amid financing controversy
  ([Wikipedia/StadiumDB][sdb1]); Côte d'Ivoire 2023 ≥~$1bn, roughly half China-linked
  ([Soccer Laduma][sl2]).
- **2026 World Cup** largely reuses existing stadiums, but host-city operating costs run
  well over $100m (Toronto ~$380m — [Global News][gn1]). **University of Toronto research:
  12 of the last 14 World Cups produced net economic losses for host cities** ([Smith][sm2]).

## §5 — Society & nature

**Gambling-ad saturation & regulation:**
- **8 of 20** Premier League clubs had front-of-shirt betting sponsors in 2024/25; clubs
  agreed to remove them from 2026/27 (first UK league to do so; ~£60m/yr at stake)
  ([Sky Sports][sky4]) — leaving a possible ~£80m shortfall ([Gambling Insider][gi1]).
- **~27,000+** gambling messages across the PL opening weekend (Aug 2025), ~3× the 2023
  level; one match = **5,262** ([ITV][itv1] / [Univ. of Bristol][bris1]).
- A 2017 study: gambling ads in **~95%** of televised matches ([BBC][bbc2]).
- Italy banned all gambling ads/sponsorship ("Decreto Dignità," 2019); Spain followed
  (2021) — Italy now weighing a reversal ([Withers][wit1]).

**Gambling harm:**
- Problem gambling ~**triples** suicide risk at 1yr, ~quadruples at 4yr in young UK adults
  (Bristol/ALSPAC, peer-reviewed — [Bristol][bris2]).
- UK govt (2023): **117–496** suicides/yr associated with problem gambling; harm cost
  ~£1.77bn ([gov.uk][gov1]) — wide-range, methodologically contested.

**Africa/Sénégal betting:** Africa's gaming industry ~**$17.6bn** in 2025 (sports-betting
revenue ~$3bn), fastest-growing worldwide; ~60m bettors (2020) → 100m+ (2025), driven by
mobile money ([allAfrica][aa1] — specify handle vs revenue). A 2024 GeoPoll survey: ~76%
had bet; concerns that 10–20% of users are under-age ([GeoPoll][gp1]). 1xBet sponsors
AFCON; kiosks proliferate in working-class Dakar ([SeneNews][sn1]).

**Labor & informal economy:**
- **Qatar worker deaths — heavily contested, do not conflate:** the Guardian's **≥6,500**
  counts *all* migrant deaths from *all* causes nationwide since 2010; Qatar's tournament
  chief acknowledged **"400–500"** on World Cup-connected projects ([CNN][cnn2]).
- Mega-events average ~**172%** cost overrun ([Clemson][clem1]); informal vendors are
  routinely displaced (Brazil 2014 barred street vendors near stadiums).
- 2026 job claims diverge wildly — FIFA-linked ~823,000 FTE vs US Soccer ~40,000 direct,
  mostly temporary (estimate/contested).

**Environment:**
- **Qatar 2022:** FIFA claimed ~**3.6 Mt CO2e** and carbon neutrality — rejected by
  scientists; Carbon Market Watch put real emissions up to ~5× higher (~18 Mt)
  ([BBC][bbc3]). Treat 3.6 Mt as a disputed lower bound.
- Qatar pitches used ~10,000–50,000 L/day each of mostly desalinated water ([Aquatech][aqua1]).
- **2026 World Cup** (48 teams, 104 matches, 16 cities/3 countries): projected most
  carbon-intensive ever — Greenly ~**7.8 Mt**, New Weather Institute **≥9 Mt up to 15 Mt**;
  **~87% from spectator/team travel**; aviation up 160–325% for lack of high-speed rail
  ([TIME][time1]). FIFA pledged 50% cuts by 2030 / net-zero 2040 ([Time][time2]) — in
  tension with the expanded format and no 2026 emissions cap.

## §6 — FairPlay Thermometer: methodology seed

A **scale-invariant** index — same instrument from World Cup to Sunday youth match —
computed **per event and per attendee**.

**Primary axis = spectator travel (Scope 3).** The evidence is overwhelming that travel
dominates:
- EURO 2024 audited footprint **316,912 t CO2e**, ~80% transport, 81% of ticket-holders
  used free public transport ([UEFA via edie][uefa1]) — **use as the one audited anchor.**
- Austrian pro football: cars carried 42.4% of fans but **71.6%** of travel GHG; ~4% who
  flew produced ~52% of travel impact (peer-reviewed — [Pereira et al.][pereira1]).

**Per-attendee benchmarks (the scale-invariance core):**
- **~6 kg CO2e/spectator** (Austrian pro football); **~7.79 kg** (German football);
  **~260 kg/person** for a mega-event ([ScienceDirect][pereira1]). The **~30–40× spread**
  between a domestic match and a mega-event *is* the central design challenge — and the
  justification for a per-attendee number.
- **Accounting frame:** ISO 14044 LCA + GHG Protocol; for travel-heavy events Scope 3 is
  ~99% (IOC methodology is the de-facto standard — [IOC][ioc1]).
- **Mode factors (DEFRA-style, per passenger-km):** intl rail ~6 g, coach ~30 g, national
  rail ~40 g, car ~60 g (4 occupants), bus ~89 g; short-haul flight far higher and the
  swing factor (pin the 2025 flight factor before publishing).

**Grassroots axis = vulnerability, not a shaky carbon total.** Grassroots emissions are
genuinely under-measured. The defensible metrics today:
- ~**120,000** English grassroots matches cancelled/yr to flooded pitches; one-third of
  pitches lose ~6 weeks/season ([Sky Sports][sky1]).
- **"Pitches in Peril"** (Football For Future + Common Goal, Sept 2025): every analysed
  pitch has crossed an unplayability threshold; Global South players already face **7×**
  more unplayable heat days; 14 of 16 World Cup 2026 stadiums exceed safe-play thresholds
  ([Football For Future][fff1]).
Build the grassroots axis around **vulnerability + cumulative fixture count**, not an
aggregate CO2 number that doesn't yet exist.

**Youth & spectator-health axis (net signal, not one-sided):**
- *Harm:* a single PL match showed **5,262** gambling messages, 91% during live play
  ([Bristol][bris1]); alcohol ads ~8.4% of broadcast time, ~10.8 min/match ([Purves][purv1]);
  matchday PM2.5 up to **10×** urban background ([ACS ES&T][acs1]).
- *Benefit (counterweight):* youth sport participation is consistently linked to higher
  self-esteem and life satisfaction, lower depression/anxiety, belonging ([systematic
  reviews][mh1]). The index must credit this so it isn't purely punitive.
